How we'd think about a matching stack

60 / 30 / 10.

Reframing the split gives a team three things at once. Lower cost, faster response times, and a matching engine that is actually debuggable when a recommendation looks wrong.

  1. 60% Traditional code and data

    Filtering, scoring, ranking, eligibility, constraints. The part of the product that should not be touching a model at all.

  2. 30% Rule-based logic

    Business rules, tie-breakers, fairness constraints, boost rules. Deterministic, explainable, and cheap to run.

  3. 10% The layer that actually needs a model

    The part where an LLM earns its keep. Smaller than the pitch deck suggests, and the piece that gets debugged first when a match looks wrong.

Pattern we've worked before

Feeld, a product company with a technical founder in a similar spot, brought us in for a scoped sprint. One workshop, a few advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture pass, and a Strategic Operations Framework their team kept using after we left.
